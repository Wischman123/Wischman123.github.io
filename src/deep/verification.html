Formal verification

How we know the answer key is right

A gate that blocks bad work is only as good as its idea of "right". Three welds pin every problem to a running simulation, and a formal-verification triangulation reconciles the authored answer key against physics computed two independent ways — one of them checked against a Lean proof.

Three welds, three artifact pairs

Three of the build's gates do one specific job: they weld a problem to a simulation. Each compares a different pair of artifacts, so each catches a failure the others cannot — merge them and the discrimination is gone.

Formal-verification triangulation

The strongest check runs three independent roads to the same number: the authored answer key, a numerical simulation, and a closed-form predictor certified against a Lean proof. When all three agree, the answer is trustworthy; when one dissents, the gate localizes which one.

The gate is not a rubber stamp

The gate is not a rubber stamp. The same three-leg machinery that reconciles the values above also runs a seeded bad-key copy of projectile-C001 whose authored range is deliberately wrong; the runner REQUIRES that run to localize a BAD_KEY (the two key-independent legs, sim and formal, agree and out-vote the authored value). A gate that could only ever go green would be evidence of nothing.

python tools/fv_triangulation_runner.py (whole-suite run: the discrimination-proof stage plus an end-to-end negative self-check on a seeded bad key)

Numbers with receipts

Verification at the scale of the whole engine, not one problem. The scene count is recounted from the scenario registry every time the site is built; the three verification figures below it have no machine-readable source in the repo yet, so they are honestly marked as hand-pinned rather than dressed up as measurements — hover any figure for its source and the day it was checked.

641† load-bearing functions property-checked — under those proved laws — not spot-checked against a saved answer
0† silently untested — every remaining function is documented infeasible-to-test, not ignored
Trust, but triangulate. A welded problem cannot disagree with the simulation that draws it; a triangulated answer key cannot disagree with the physics twice over.
